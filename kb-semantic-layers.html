Semantic Layers & Ontologies

The bridge between raw data and business meaning

A semantic layer translates raw database tables into business concepts that humans understand. Instead of asking "what's the sum of revenue_usd where status = 'completed' and date >= '2024-01-01'", you ask "what's our Q1 revenue?" An ontology takes this further—defining not just metrics, but the relationships between concepts: customers place orders, orders contain products, products belong to categories.

This is one of the most important (and underrated) areas in modern data work. Get it right, and your data becomes self-documenting, AI-ready, and actually useful to the business.

Why This Matters Now

The AI connection: LLMs hallucinate with data because they lack business context. A semantic layer provides that context—making your data AI-ready without endless prompt engineering.

  • Single source of truth — Define "revenue" once, use it everywhere
  • Self-service analytics — Business users can explore without SQL knowledge
  • AI-ready data — LLMs can reason over semantically-rich data
  • Governance — Clear ownership of metric definitions
  • Consistency — No more "my numbers don't match your numbers"

Key Concepts

Semantic Layer

A business abstraction over raw data. Maps database columns to business terms. Defines how metrics are calculated. Lives between your data warehouse and consumption tools.

Metrics Layer

A subset focused specifically on metric definitions. Revenue, churn rate, conversion rate—defined once, calculated consistently everywhere.

Ontology

A formal model of concepts in your domain and their relationships. Goes beyond metrics to define entities (Customer, Product, Order) and how they relate.

Knowledge Graph

The ontology brought to life with real data. A graph database where nodes are entities and edges are relationships. Enables complex reasoning and discovery.

Standards & Specifications

RDF

Resource Description Framework. W3C standard for data interchange using subject-predicate-object triples.

OWL

Web Ontology Language. For representing rich, complex knowledge about things, groups, and relationships.

SPARQL

Query language for RDF data. Like SQL for graph data.

SML

Semantic Modeling Language. AtScale's open standard for portable metric definitions (Apache licensed).

My Notes & Observations

The convergence is real

Semantic layers (business metrics) and ontologies (entity relationships) are converging. Tools like Timbr blur the line. The end state is probably a unified "business knowledge layer" that handles both.

Start with metrics, grow into ontology

Don't boil the ocean. Start with a metrics layer (Cube or dbt) for your core KPIs. Add ontological richness later as your AI/search use cases demand it.

Warehouse-native is interesting but early

Snowflake and Databricks are building native semantic layers. Watch this space—but for now, dedicated tools are more mature.

The AI angle is not hype

Every RAG implementation I've seen struggles with business context. A semantic layer solves this. LLMs + semantic layer = dramatically better results than LLMs + raw tables.
